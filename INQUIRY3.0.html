Inquiry And Analysis

What is the problem?

HIV stands for human immunodeficiency virus and is a virus that can lead to the acquired immunodeficiency syndrome, or AIDS. Although you can live with HIV and AIDS, like cancer, there is no cure yet. However, there are still multiple deaths in many countries. For example, in 2005, there were close to 5 million HIV infections and 3 million deaths due to aids. Under developed countries such as Lesotho and other countires in south africa suffer heavily from HIV and AIDS.

Research Plan

Firstly, we need to recognize that finding a cure for HIV and AIDS at this point in time is hard. However, we can find the cause and effect of this which can also lead to more scientific research, ultimately finding a cure for HIV and eventually aids. Firstly, we must start with the cause and the effect of HIV. The cause of HIV can be many things. It can be spread by contact with infected blood or from mother to child during pregnancy, chilbirth or breast feeding. Without medication, it may take years before HIV weakens your immune system. After this, you will get AIDS. Aids is a chronic, potentially life threatening disease that kids and adults do NOT want to have.

After finding the cause and effect, we need to find out the countries most effected by HIV and AIDS. Obviously countires such as Lesotho and other under-devloped countries are the ones that will have the most people affected by HIV and AIDS.

After finding the countries with the most PERCENTAGE (some small countries with a small population could have a high percentage of people affected by HIV and AIDS) of people affected by HIV and AIDS. After this, we can study what they do in their everyday lives, who they generally interact wiht, as well as their birth proccess. We can compare these statistics to one of the more healthy countries.
